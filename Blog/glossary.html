Social Media, Cyberbullying, and Online Safety Glossary

In our Glossary below, we define the terms you need to know in the realm of social media, cyberbullying, and online safety, so that you are increasingly informed about technological jargon as you work with the youth under your care.

Direct Message (DM): A private message sent on social media platforms.

Emoji: A small digital image or icon used to express an idea, emotion, etc., in electronic communication.

Engagement: The number of interactions a post receives on social media platforms.

Filter: A feature on social media platforms that allows users to alter the appearance of their photos.

Follow: A feature on social media platforms that allows users to subscribe to another user’s posts.

Followers: Users who have subscribed to another user’s posts on social media platforms.

Following: Users who have subscribed to another user’s posts on social media platforms.

Friend: A user who has accepted another user’s friend request on social media platforms.

Geotag: A feature on social media platforms that allows users to tag their location in a post.

Hashtag: A word or phrase preceded by a hash or pound sign (#) and used to identify messages on a specific topic.

Impression: The number of times a post is displayed on social media platforms.

Instagram: A social media platform that allows users to share photos and videos.

Like: A feature on social media platforms that allows users to show appreciation for a post.

Link: A feature on social media platforms that allows users to share a URL in a post.

Live: A feature on social media platforms that allows users to broadcast a video in real-time.

Log in: The process of accessing a computer system or software by identifying and authenticating oneself.

Log out: The process of exiting a computer system or software.

Message: A private message sent on social media platforms.

News Feed: A feature on social media platforms that displays a user’s posts and the posts of users they follow.

Notification: A feature on social media platforms that alerts users to new activity on their account.

Influencer: An individual who can sway an audience through a digital platform. This term is often used in relation to social media marketing, promotion, and other related efforts.

Streak: A feature on some social media platforms, like Snapchat, that counts the consecutive days users have sent messages to each other.

Tweet: A post made on the social media platform Twitter.

Post: Content shared by a user on their social media profile.

Profile: A user's personal page on a social media platform where their information, posts, and interactions are displayed.

Tag: A feature on social media platforms that allows users to mention other users or highlight keywords in a post.

Timeline: A chronological display of a user’s posts on social media platforms.

Trending: A term used to describe topics or hashtags that are currently popular on social media platforms.

Unfollow: A feature that allows users to stop subscribing to another user’s posts.

Viral: A term used to describe content that rapidly gains popularity and spreads widely across social media platforms.

Story: A feature on social media platforms that allows users to share photos and videos that disappear after 24 hours.

Repost: Sharing someone else's content on your own social media profile.

Mention: A feature that allows users to tag another user in a post or comment, often using the "@" symbol.

Bio: A short description of a user, typically found on their profile page.

Handle: A user’s unique username on a social media platform, often preceded by the "@" symbol.

Feed: The main page on social media platforms where users see posts from accounts they follow.

Story Highlights: A feature on Instagram that allows users to save their stories on their profile past the 24-hour expiration.

Pinned Post: A post that a user chooses to keep at the top of their profile for extended visibility.
