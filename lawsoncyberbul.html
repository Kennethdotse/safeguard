Cybersecurity Act 2020: Building a Secure Digital Space

Ghana's Cybersecurity Act of 2020 (Act 1038) is a key piece of legislation designed to protect the country's growing digital landscape. Here's a summary of its key points:
Establishing the Cyber Security Authority (CSA): The Act creates the CSA, responsible for regulating cybersecurity activities, promoting cybersecurity development, and handling cyber threats.
Protecting Critical Infrastructure: The Act identifies critical infrastructure essential to national security and economic well-being. It mandates registration and cybersecurity measures for owners of such infrastructure.
Cybersecurity Fund: The Act establishes a fund to support cybersecurity initiatives, including public awareness campaigns and capacity building.
International Cooperation: The Act emphasizes collaboration with other countries on cybercrime and cybersecurity issues. It establishes a 24/7 contact point to facilitate international cooperation.
Promoting Cybersecurity Awareness: The Act encourages public awareness about cybersecurity threats and best practices. The Cybersecurity Act 2020 strengthens Ghana's defenses against cybercrime and fosters a more secure digital environment.

Your Privacy Protected: Ghana's Data Protection Act (2012)

The Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) is a Ghanaian law that safeguards your right to privacy by regulating how organizations handle your personal information.,
Data Protection Commission Established: This government body oversees enforcement of the Act and ensures businesses comply with its regulations.
Control Over Your Data: You have the right to access, rectify any errors in, and request deletion of your personal information held by organizations.
Eight Data Principles: These principles dictate how businesses can collect, use, and store your data. They include obtaining your consent and maintaining robust security measures to protect your information.
The Act empowers you to control your personal information and ensures businesses handle it responsibly.
